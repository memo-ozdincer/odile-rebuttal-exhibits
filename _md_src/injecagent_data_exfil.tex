\section*{InjecAgent --- data-stealing (exfiltration) attacker instructions (verbatim from the benchmark)}

\footnotesize
The \textbf{32 unique data-stealing (\texttt{ds}) attacker instructions} --- the harder class, and the one a shape-based defense cannot catch. Every attack is a \emph{two-tool chain}: read something private (saved payment methods, genetic data, medical records, stored passwords, web-search history) and exfiltrate it through an ordinary-looking \tool{GmailSendEmail} to the attacker's address (\texttt{amy.watson@gmail.com}). There is no ``ignore your instructions'' trigger and no malformed token; the surface form is indistinguishable from a benign ``retrieve X and email it to me.'' That is exactly why retrieval-shaped exfiltration defeats input filters. ODILE drives both \texttt{ds\_base} and \texttt{ds\_enhanced} to $0.00\%$ ASR at Llama-3.3-70B.
