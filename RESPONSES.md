## FoEk (Rating 3)

We thank the reviewer - generalization, attack strength, capability breadth, and model recency are the right axes, and we ran new experiments on each.

**1 · "Data leakage - is ODILE a real defense or fitting AgentDojo?"**
To show ODILE learns the harmful-intent signal rather than the AgentDojo test set, we evaluate it on benchmarks and attack-augmentations absent from training. The single clearest result: on **InjecAgent** - a different benchmark, different (ReAct) format, n=1054/family - ODILE drives ASR to **≤0.15% on every one of 7 backbones** ([InjecAgent table](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/T3b_injecagent_crossmodel.pdf)). A benchmark-fitted adapter cannot do this. These InjecAgent attacks share no surface form, domain, or tooling with ODILE's AgentDojo training injections - they are genetic-data exfiltration, smart-lock access, Dropbox file-moves, e-commerce/email attacks, not banking money-transfers. You can scroll the actual strings side-by-side: [124 real InjecAgent attacks](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/examples/injecagent_attacks.md) vs [the AgentDojo injections ODILE trained on](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/examples/agentdojo_train_injections.md). The same holds on **TensorTrust** (a different mechanic - guardian-bot password extraction; extract-leak 78–92%→1–44%, [table](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/T4_TT_headline.pdf), [91 real attacks](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/examples/tensortrust_attacks.md)), **WASP** (0/239 verified-tight, [table](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/tab_T_wasp.pdf)), **AgentDyn** ([a dynamic benchmark on 3 non-AgentDojo suites](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/examples/agentdyn_difference.md); base hijacked 30.0% → ODILE ≈0, preliminary), and on [AgentDojo attack-augmentations absent from training](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/examples/agentdojo_heldout_augmentations.md) (the same goal rendered marker-removed / base64 - surface forms the adapter never saw at train time).

**2 · "Attack setting too weak - no adapter-aware adaptive attacker."**
We add adaptive attackers covering every internal a defense-aware attacker could target. White-box **GCG** and a **LoRA-/layer-aware GCG** that optimizes against the adapter at its exact layers both fail (0/4 and 0/3): the attacker's target cross-entropy never leaves ~10.7, while on the *same* pairs the undefended base descends to a compliant ~0.57 and cracks - so the attack works, ODILE is what stops it ([table](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/T_GCG.pdf)). Two objective-aware semantic attackers, **TAP and PAIR** (given ODILE's objective), get **0/64** across two backbones - 0/48 at the standard tree-search budget plus 0/16 when every non-crack is re-run at a larger budget - against base **36/48** and a sanitizer firewall **15/48** on the identical cells ([table](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/tab_adaptive_tap_pair.pdf)); base and sanitizer cracking those exact cells is the positive control. The masking strategy is a training-time loss localization, so there is nothing input-side to route around. (That covers the four axes you named - adapter-, layer-range-, objective-, and masking-aware.)

**3 · "Benign capability evaluation narrow - BFCL, Tau-Bench."**
We add exactly those two: **BFCL** (n=1240/cell, ≈flat) and **τ-bench airline**, confirming retained general tool-use ([table](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/T6_auxiliary_benign.pdf)).

**4 · "Model selection outdated - newer reasoning models."**
We add **Qwen3-Next-80B-A3B-Thinking** (Sept 2025), a frontier reasoning model with nonstandard (Gated-DeltaNet) attention; ODILE transfers with the attention-path adjustments ([Qwen3-Next table](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/tab_pareto_qnext.pdf), [cross-model](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/T2_crossmodel_AD.pdf)). Full grid camera-ready.

**Q · "Why LoRA on layers 30–55?"**
Not trial-and-error: following circuit-breakers (Zou et al.), mid-to-late layers avoid token-specific representations; we use that single principled band and depth-scale it across all 7 backbones with no per-backbone retuning - which is itself why the recipe transfers. A full layer-band ablation is a camera-ready commitment.

## FSAe (Rating 7)

Thank you for the careful read.

**(a/b) Worsened pairs - how to handle / prior work.** These three banking pairs are structurally ambiguous: the attacker IBAN equals the legitimate recipient, so the harmful and benign twins are byte-identical and give the contrastive objective no signal. Prior non-paired methods don't surface this - it's specific to paired-trace construction. We handle it by a structural exclusion (target-collision in the task definition), not a per-sweep statistic.
**(c) ASR including them.** Pure downside: AD-standard ASR +0.18pp, benign −0.37pp; every worsened sample traces to that one task. Camera-ready: a stacking section - deterministic post-execution validators (e.g. IBAN allowlists) are the right complement for this genuinely-ambiguous class.

**Qwen MSE > Qwen Triplet - why?** Both objectives drive ASR→≈0, so the security result isn't loss-specific; the deviation is scale-sensitivity. The triplet variant uses **absolute-distance margins**; the canonical objective is **direction-only (cosine), scale-invariant**. Qwen-2.5/Qwen3 sit at a different residual-stream magnitude scale than Llama - massive activations (Sun et al. 2024), attention sinks in the targeted layers, and Qwen3's QK-Norm - so Llama-calibrated margins mis-fire on Qwen while the cosine objective is invariant to those norms. That is why the cosine objective is the headline and triplet the ablation (Zou et al. 2024, circuit-breakers, use a cosine loss for the same reason).

**Layer range - trial-and-error?** No: the mid-late band is the circuit-breaker rationale (Zou et al.), depth-scaled and transfer-validated across 7 backbones. A full native layer-band sweep is a camera-ready commitment.

**Whitebox/blackbox "different landscape."** Agreed - we will state explicitly that ODILE is an inner defense for the open-weight / self-hosted regime, complementary to (not competing with) blackbox/pipeline defenses for API-only settings. Thank you for the framing.

## DefG (Rating 4)

**1 · Compare vs Meta-SecAlign + firewall; clarify novelty.**
*Novelty:* ODILE is a representation-level LoRA at **1× inference cost** - no second model pass, no input rewriting - trained by a paired-trace contrast on the model's own harmful direction.
*vs Meta-SecAlign* ([headline](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/T1_70B_headline.pdf)): we hold the low-ASR corner (0.01% vs 2.11%) at 1× cost. The sharpest single piece of evidence is a **matched-backbone trace**: on the same banking task at 70B, Meta-SecAlign reads the injected bill and **executes `send_money` to the attacker IBAN** (defense cracked, 13/144 on this attack family), while ODILE's `send_money` recipient degenerates into token-soup that never resolves to the attacker - 0/144 ([trace](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/traces/agentdojo_injecagent_banking_secalign_vs_odile.md)). The mechanism behind it is **format robustness** - Meta-SecAlign is marker-keyed, so it breaks under format shift: on the marker-removed augmentation it leaks 3.38% where ODILE holds 0.00% ([table](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/t_ad_injection_styles.pdf)), and ODILE jams identically across ReAct vs delimiter formats ([figure](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/fig_react_jam_odile.pdf); InjecAgent ReAct ASR ≤0.15% every backbone).
*vs firewall/sanitizer* ([table](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/T_firewall.pdf)): static parity (2.1% ASR), but the sanitizer costs −6.2pp benign utility plus an extra LLM call per tool output, and is **cracked 15/48 under adaptive attack where ODILE holds 0/64** ([table](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/tab_adaptive_tap_pair.pdf)). ODILE is preferable when adaptive robustness, 1× cost, and benign preservation matter together.

**2 · Table 2 hard to interpret + UAU under-discussed.**
Agreed. We replace Table 2 with **one table per backbone - same model, same metric pair (ASR vs benign utility)**: [70B](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/tab_pareto_70b.pdf), [8B](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/tab_pareto_8b.pdf), [Qwen-2.5-7B](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/tab_pareto_q25_7b.pdf), [Qwen-2.5-14B](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/tab_pareto_q25_14b.pdf), [Qwen3-32B](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/tab_pareto_q3_32b.pdf). On under-attack utility: we report **benign (no-attack) utility** as the capability axis; UAU is appendix-by-design, because ODILE jams under injection (it removes the attacker tool call), so UAU measures residual non-malicious throughput, not task completion. We will make the benign-vs-UAU distinction explicit in the body.

**3 · Benchmark-specific (mostly AgentDojo).** Same generalization evidence as above: InjecAgent + TensorTrust + WASP + AgentDyn, all ASR≈0 with the same recipe, on benchmarks where the attack genuinely lands on base (e.g. AgentDyn base 30.0% → ODILE ≈0) ([InjecAgent](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/T3b_injecagent_crossmodel.pdf), [TensorTrust](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/T4_TT_headline.pdf), [WASP](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/tab_T_wasp.pdf)).

**4 · Brittle - garbled tool calls; real loop with retries/validators?** The jam is the design intent, not a side effect, and you can read exactly what it looks like: on the same task where base calls `update_password(password="new_password")`, ODILE reads the file and emits token-soup with **no parseable tool call** ([trace](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/traces/agentdojo_banking_direct_user12_base_vs_odile.md)). In a real loop, that means the retry-on-error handler fires and the model is re-prompted with the injection not re-supplied - the strict-deny endpoint. Critically, the jam never *accidentally* produces the attacker action: across the adaptive sweep the agent emits no attacker tool call on any cell (0/5805 on WASP, trace-validated; [TAP/PAIR](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/tab_adaptive_tap_pair.pdf)), and where it *does* emit a `send_money`, the recipient argument is degenerate and never resolves to the attacker IBAN ([trace](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/traces/agentdojo_injecagent_banking_secalign_vs_odile.md)). Deterministic post-execution validators stack naturally on top.

**Q · Data-exfiltration that looks like normal retrieval.** Yes - InjecAgent's ds_base/ds_enhanced families are exactly exfiltration via a legitimate-looking tool call (retrieve X → email/send to attacker); you can scroll the [32 real data-stealing attacks](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/examples/injecagent_data_exfil.md) (genetic data, saved addresses, financial records → `GmailSendEmail` to the attacker). ODILE drives both families to 0.00% at 70B ([InjecAgent](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/T3b_injecagent_crossmodel.pdf), [per-family](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/t_injecagent_full.pdf)). A matched trace shows the contrast on a Slack exfiltration cell: base reads the private channels and `post_webpage`s them to the attacker; ODILE jams with no channel read and no post ([trace](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/traces/agentdojo_injecagent_slack_user0_base_vs_odile.md)).

**Minor · Figure 2 legend.** We will declutter and split the Llama/Qwen panels.

## GHCC (Rating 5)

**1 · Limited applicability to closed models.** Fair - ODILE is an inner defense for the open-weight / self-hosted regime (the proposal is for anyone self-hosting); API-only deployments use pipeline defenses, which ODILE complements. We will add an explicit deployment-scope paragraph.

**2 · Limited model coverage / justify Qwen-2.5.** Six backbones plus **Qwen3-Next-80B**, including Qwen3-8B/32B from the Qwen-3 family. Qwen3-Next is the newest reasoning model the "newer Qwen such as Qwen3" ask points to; ODILE transfers ([cross-model](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/T2_crossmodel_AD.pdf), [Qwen3-Next](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/tab_pareto_qnext.pdf)).

**3 · Single-benchmark - AgentDyn, WASP.** We add both you name: **AgentDyn** - a separate dynamic benchmark on three suites that don't exist in AgentDojo (GitHub, Daily-Life, Shopping; [how it differs + real tasks/goals](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/examples/agentdyn_difference.md)), where the attack genuinely hijacks the base agent (**30.0% ASR**, n=80, trace-validated `send_money`/checkout cracks) and ODILE drives it to **≈0** (0 cracks so far; preliminary, full trace-validated sweep in progress) - and **WASP** (0/239 verified-tight, deepening to 0/5805 per paraphrase attempt; our setup runs WASP's [21 canonical attacker goals + injection templates](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/examples/wasp_attacker_goals.md) without the live browser loop - the full containerized version is camera-ready, [table](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/tab_T_wasp.pdf)), alongside InjecAgent + TensorTrust.

**4 · No adaptive attack (adapter/objective/layer/masking-aware).** All four axes: adapter- and layer-range-aware (LoRA-aware GCG, 0/3), objective-aware (TAP+PAIR, 0/64), masking-aware (the mask is train-time - nothing to route around), plus discrete GCG (0/4) and WASP (0/5805) ([GCG](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/T_GCG.pdf), [TAP/PAIR](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/tab_adaptive_tap_pair.pdf)).

**Q1 · What does "88% benign retention" mean?** It was one specific axis (InjecAgent calls-user-tool rate at 70B, base = 100%). We now report **absolute numbers with ASR / benign utility separated, per backbone** ([tables](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/tab_pareto_70b.pdf)) - exactly your ask.

**Q2 · Where does the 12% drop come from?** Of the 12/100: **0 malformed tool calls, 0 incomplete chains, 0 wrong answers, 0 refusals - all 12 are format-envelope shifts** (strict-ReAct `Action:` → semantically-equivalent expression; tool-call content correct in every case). So it is metric-strictness, not capability loss; we will list all 12 in the appendix.

**Q3 · Position vs CaMeL.** CaMeL is a design-level defense (privileged/quarantined planner + interpreter) - a different stack layer, complementary. We evaluate it at three scales: it scale-floors at Qwen-2.5-14B (the planner cannot drive the interpreter, so its 0% ASR is vacuous), runs on 2/4 suites at Llama-3.3-70B (the others hit an upstream interpreter bug), and completes at Gemini-2.5-flash-lite at ~0% ASR but only 18–32% benign utility. CaMeL reaches ~0% ASR where its interpreter runs, but is brittle and high-tax on open weights; ODILE reaches 0% at 1× cost on every backbone.

**Q4 · When is ODILE preferable to simpler sanitization/firewall defenses?** Static parity on ASR, but the sanitizer costs benign utility plus an extra LLM call per tool output and is cracked 15/48 under adaptive attack where ODILE holds 0/64 ([firewall](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/T_firewall.pdf), [adaptive](https://github.com/memo-ozdincer/odile-rebuttal-exhibits/blob/main/tab_adaptive_tap_pair.pdf)). ODILE is preferable when adaptive robustness, zero extra inference cost, and benign-utility preservation matter together - the realistic agent-deployment setting.
